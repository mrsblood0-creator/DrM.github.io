Suvorexant: Mechanism of Action, Therapeutic Uses & Adverse Effects

Overview

  • Famous trade name: Belsomra
  • It was first synthesized in 2006
  • Suvorexant is an orexin receptor antagonist that is used in treatment of insomnia

Orexin

  • Orexin 1 and 2 are peptides which are available in some neurons in the hypothalamus and their function is promote wakefulness
  • When there is higher level of orexin, those neurons get activated to keep the body awake, but when their levels falls then this lead to sleep
  • Orexin receptor antagonists (suvorexant) work by antagonizing orexin so the body gets to sleep
  • Suvorexant work by antagonizing both orexin 1 and 2 receptors

Therapeutic uses

  • Suvorexant is FDA approved for treatment of sleep onset and sleep maintenance insomnia

Adverse effects

  1. Next day somnolence similar to narcolepsy
    • Narcolepsy is a disease that is characterized by loss of the orexin neurons leading to day time sleepiness, when antagonizing orexin, similar effect to narcolepsy happen
  2. Headache, dizziness, abnormal dreams
  3. Dry mouth
  4. Rare: Sleep paralysis, hallucinations, complex sleep behaviors and suicidal ideation
    • Tolerance and dependence doesn’t occur with suvorexant

Contraindications

  • Suvorexant is contraindicated in people with narcolepsy

Pharmacokinetics

  • Available as oral formula only
  • Absorption is good with bioavailability of 82%
  • It is metabolized by CYP3A4 enzymes in the liver, and it is liable to be affected by CYP3A4 inducers and inhibitors
  • Elimination half life is around 12 hours
  • Excretion: 60% through bile and the rest in urine
