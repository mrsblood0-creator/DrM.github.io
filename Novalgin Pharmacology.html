Novalgin Pharmacology: Mechanism of Action, Therapeutic Uses & Adverse Effects

Classification of analgesics

  1. Opioid analgesics
  2. NSAIDs
  3. Analgesics antipyretics drugs: e.g. paracetamol, Novalgine and nefopam
  4. Non analgesic drugs that work as analgesics for some conditions: e.g. carbamazepine (antiepileptic drug) used for trigeminal neuralgia, duloxetine (anti depressant) used for diabetic neuropathy

Overview

  • Scientific name is metamizole or dipyrone, trade name is Novalgin
  • it is an analgesic, antispasmodic and antipyretic medication
  • It was first synthesized in 1922 in Germany
  • Novalgin is banned in some countries due to studies suggesting severe drug adverse effects such as agranulocytosis

Pharmacokinetics

  • It is available in oral, IM and IV formulations
  • Oral formula is well absorbed with 100% bioavailability
  • Metabolized by liver and excreted in urine and feces
  • Elimination half life is 14 minutes for parent compound and 2-4 hours for its metabolites (active metabolites)

Mechanism of action

  • MOA is unknown, theory: it work by inhibiting brain and spinal cord prostaglandins to relieve pain and fever, it also work as antispasmodic

Therapeutic uses

  • It is used as analgesic for moderate to severe pain, e.g. peri and post operative pain, colic, cancer pain and trauma related pain
  • it has more potent analgesia than aspirin but it has no anti inflammatory action

Adverse effects

  • Agranulocytosis and aplastic anemia (bone marrow suppression)
  • Compared to NSAIDS, Novalgin causes less kidney, CVS and GIT side effects
  • It may trigger bronchospasm or anaphylaxis (especially in asthma)
  • It also trigger another hypersensitivity reactions like toxic epidermal necrolysis because it is chemically related to sulfonamides

Contraindications

  • Previous hypersensitivity reactions to the same drug or to sulfonamides
  • Impaired hematopoiesis (e.g. during chemotherapy)
  • Third trimester of pregnancy and lactation
  • Children with body weight less than 16 kg
