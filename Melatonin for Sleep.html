Melatonin for Sleep: Mechanism of Action & Side Effects

Melatonin

  • Melatonin is a hormone that is produced by the pineal gland in the brain and its main function is related to regulating sleep- wake cycle and circadian rhythm
  • Darkness triggers melatonin production, which when secreted gives hypnotic effect (sleep effect), light decrease production of the hormone
  • Insomnia became problematic when artificial light was discovered, artificial light decreased melatonin levels
  • In the early 2000s, comes the idea of using this hormone as a medication or a supplement to treat insomnia

Therapeutic Uses

  • Melatonin is used as a supplement for treatment of insomnia and improving sleep
  • It is also used to overcome jet lag
  • REM sleep behavior disorders

Mechanism of action

  • Melatonin act as agonist on melatonin receptors including MT1, MT2 in the hypothalamus to mediate sleep and regulates circadian rhythm

Pharmacokinetics

  • Available as oral formulas only
  • Good absorption with bioavailability of 2- 50%
  • Distribution: melatonin is bound to plasma proteins by 60%
  • It is metabolized by CYP enzymes in the liver and it is liable to be affected by CYP inducers and inhibitors
  • Elimination: 90% eliminated through urine and the rest in bile and elimination half life is about 30 minutes

Side effects

  • Dizziness
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea
  • Exacerbated insomnia
  • Hypertension
  • Abdominal pain
  • Heartburn
  • Mouth ulcers
  • Aggression
  • Pruritus
  • Glycosuria
  • Increased weight
  • Mood swings
  • Tiredness
  • Abnormal liver function test

Contraindications

  • It is contraindicated in Previous hypersensitivity reactions
  • It is not recommended in autoimmune diseases, pregnancy, breastfeeding and liver diseases
