Codeine Pharmacology: Mechanism of Action, Therapeutic Uses & Adverse Effects

  • Opiate from phenanthrenes derived from opium plant
  • Codeine is prodrug of morphine (methyl morphine)
  • It is the most commonly used opioid

Pharmacokinetics

  • Mainly available as oral formula
  • Good absorption with Oral bioavailability of 90% which is higher than the morphine bioavailability due to less first pass effect (due to the methyl group in the codeine make it lipid soluble )
  • Metabolized by liver
  • Excreted through renal and biliary routes
  • It can work by two ways, either it works as methylmorphine and activate opioid receptors or work as a morphine when it gets metabolized by the liver
  • Conversion to morphine is variable in different people, the average is 10%

Mechanism of action

  • Codeine work as agonist on the Mu (Î¼) opioid receptors

Pharmacologic Effects

  • Activation of these opioid receptors lead to several CNS effects:
    • Analgesia
    • Euphoria
    • Sedation
    • Respiratory depression
    • Cough suppression
    • Miosis
    • Nausea and vomiting
    • Pruritus
  • Activation of these opioid receptors lead to several peripheral effects including:
    • CVS
    • GIT
    • RS
    • Renal
    • Endocrine
    • Immune
  • Analgesic potency is 10% of the morphine potency and it is variable in different people depending on the capacity of converting the drug into morphine
  • More potent cough suppression
  • It has little euphoric effect compared to morphine
  • Most of the systemic effects of codeine are due to conversion in the liver into morphine by demethylation because codeine is methylmorphine

Therapeutic uses

  • Analgesic for mild to moderate pain, combined with paracetamol or NSAIDs or aspirin to give better analgesia
  • Central antitussive (anti cough), not used in children younger than 12
  • Used to treat diarrhea, but there is better alternatives like loperamide and diphenoxylate

Adverse effects

  • Less than morphine
  • Mainly constipation
  • Respiratory center depression
  • Physical dependence
  • Tolerance and Addiction
