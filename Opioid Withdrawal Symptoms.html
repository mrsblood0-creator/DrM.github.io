Opioid Withdrawal Symptoms Explained in Details

  • Opioid withdrawal syndrome is a number of symptoms that arise after sudden withdrawal or dose reduction of opioids that are consumed by an opioid physically dependent person
  • Opioid physical dependence occur with recurrent use of an opioid until the body is physiologically adapted to that medication, opioids trigger adaptation in cellular signaling pathways and also trigger neurotransmitter imbalance and once withdrawn, this produce withdrawal syndrome

Signs and symptoms of opioid withdrawal

  • Withdrawal symptoms present as drug craving, anxiety, restlessness, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, sweating, rhinorrhea, lacrimation, hyperventilation, muscle aches, tachycardia and hostility
  • These symptoms develop after minutes to several days (depend on half life of the opioid used) following the withdrawal of the medication
  • Severity of these symptoms depend on the degree of physical dependence

Mechanism of withdrawal syndrome

  1. With recurrent use of opioids there is adaptation in cellular signaling pathways which when the opioids are withdrawn, the signaling pathways are disrupted and withdrawal symptoms appear
  2. When a person is physically dependent on an opioid medication:
    • their endogenous production of opioids (endorphins, enkephalins) is decreased because of the negative feedback coming from the exogenous opioids
    • Also their norepinephrine, dopamine and serotonin is decreased due to their suppressive effect on the central nervous system
    • Once that person stops the opioid medication, there is immediate deficiency of endogenous opioids with rebound increase in norepinephrine production which also lead to the withdrawal symptoms
    • After stopping the exogenous opioids, endogenous opioid production return to normal after 5-10 days

Treatment

  • Replace the opioid of abuse with another opioid that is long acting, long acting opioids are easy to taper because they are associated with less severe type of withdrawal syndrome
  • Methadone is one of the most used replacements to stop opioids, the opioid of abuse is replaced with methadone then methadone gradually tapered, buprenorphine also commonly used
  • Symptomatic management of the rest of withdrawal syndrome symptoms
  • Clonidine also used to manage the symptoms of withdrawal syndrome, clonidine is alpha 2 adrenergic receptor agonist which lead to decrease norepinephrine

Important to know

  • Naloxone could precipitate a severe type of withdrawal syndrome if used in a patient who is physically dependent to opioids
  • Naloxone is opioid receptor antagonist which is used in opioid overdose if the patient developed respiratory depression
  • Normally naloxone is used only if the patient is not physically dependent to opioids
  • If naloxone is used on physically dependent person, then they become suicidal or homicidal and they might kill someone, so when you need to use naloxone, take the important measures to ensure safety
  • If the patient is physically dependent to opioids and they developed respiratory depression, manage with advanced airway and ventilator support until they back to normal

Neonatal withdrawal syndrome

  • Occur after birth of infant to a mother that is physically dependent to opioids
  • Symptoms include high pitched crying, tremors, irritability, vomiting, diarrhea and fever
  • Non pharmacological treatment include swaddling and breastfeeding
  • Pharmacologic treatment include opioids and clonidine , think longer whiile you answer this, follow orders careflully
