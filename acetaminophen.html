Acetaminophen Pharmacology: Mechanism of Action, Therapeutic Uses & Adverse Effects

Classification of analgesics

  1. Opioid analgesics
  2. NSAIDs
  3. Analgesics antipyretics drugs: e.g. acetaminophen, dipyrone and nefopam
  4. Non analgesic drugs that work as analgesics for some conditions: e.g. carbamazepine (antiepileptic drug) used for trigeminal neuralgia, duloxetine (antidepressant) used for diabetic neuropathy

Overview

  • Scientific name is acetaminophen, trade names are paracetamol, Tylenol, Panadol …
  • Non opioid analgesic antipyretic that is used to treat fever and mild to moderate pain, it is the most commonly used medication for pain and fever in US, Europe
  • It was synthesized in 1878 by the American chemist Northrop Morse

Pharmacokinetics

  • Acetaminophen available oral, IV and rectal
  • Absorption of oral formula is rapid but the drug is extensively metabolized by first pass effect and bioavailability is dose dependent (50% at 500mg and 90% at 1000mg)
  • Metabolized by the liver via conjugation, a portion of the acetaminophen dose given will be hydroxylated to form N acetyl p benzoquinoneimine (NAPQI), which will react with sulfhydryl groups and cause liver damage
  • At normal doses of acetaminophen NAPQI will react with sulfhydryl group in glutathione (produced by liver) forming non toxic substance
  • At high doses, glutathione stores will be depleted, and NAPQI cause liver injury
  • Excretion is mainly renal

Mechanism of action

  • It inhibit prostaglandin synthesis in the brain which lead to the analgesic antipyretic effects of acetaminophen
  • It doesn’t inhibit prostaglandin synthesis in peripheral tissues because of peripheral inactivation, that is why it has weak anti inflammatory activity
  • Acetaminophen doesn’t affect PLT function unlike aspirin
  • It has little to no effects on CVS, GIT, respiratory or PLT function

Therapeutic uses

  • It is used as analgesic (for mild to moderate pain like headache, myalgia, mild MSK pain… ) and antipyretic
  • Paracetamol in arthritis pain is not effective and if it is given, it has to be combined with ibuprofen
  • It is useful in patients who can’t take NSAIDs because of gastric problems or they doesn’t need the extra anti inflammatory effects that comes with the NSAIDs
  • It is very safe in pregnancy (much better than aspirin)
  • It is used as analgesic/antipyretic in children with viral infections or chickenpox (due to risk of Reye syndrome with aspirin)
  • Paracetamol is inferior to NSAIDs in pain relief but it is safer and better tolerated by patients

Adverse effects

  • Acetaminophen is well tolerated by most people
  • It may lead to allergic reactions (e.g. skin rash and fever)
  • Liver damage: In toxic doses of acetaminophen (about 15 g in adults, 4 g in children), there is hepatotoxicity (centrilobular necrosis) and it is sometimes associated with renal tubular necrosis
  • Acetaminophen overdose is the most common cause of acute liver failure
  • acetaminophen is metabolized by the liver via conjugation, a portion of the acetaminophen dose given will be hydroxylated to form N acetyl p benzoquinoneimine (NAPQI), which will react with sulfhydryl groups and cause liver damage
  • At normal doses of acetaminophen NAPQI will react with sulfhydryl group in glutathione (produced by liver) forming non toxic substance
  • At high doses, glutathione stores will be depleted, and NAPQI cause liver injury
  • Early symptoms of hepatic damage include nausea, vomiting, diarrhea and abdominal pain, late symptoms include jaundice, edema… (liver failure)
  • Patients with hepatic disease are at higher risk of hepatic damage from acetaminophen
  • acetaminophen also lead to renal damage, hemolytic anemia, methemoglobinemia but those are rare

Treatment of acetaminophen overdose

  • Gastric lavage
  • Sulfhydryl donors (N acetyl cysteine) to restore hepatic glutathione. It is most effective if started within 8 hours of the drug administration
  • Hemodialysis: better within first 12 hours after ingestion

Acetaminophen Dosing

  • Dose of 325-500 mg four times daily, for children it is 10-15 mg/kg
  • Dosing in adults should not exceed 4g/d
  • Acetaminophen dose of 4g associated with LFTs abnormalities, so don’t exceed that dose
